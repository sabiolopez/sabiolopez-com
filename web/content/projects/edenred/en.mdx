context: "The project starts from the observation that the digital hiring flow represented only an operational snapshot of the actual buying journey. The experience ignored critical evaluation stages, internal validation, and trust-building typical of the HR decision-maker. The goal was to reorganize the journey considering real user behavior and the interaction between marketing, portal, and assisted channels.\n\nDuring the project period, the Growth area was being consolidated and the B2B portal was treated as the main digital hiring channel. The existing journey reflected a fragmented operational logic. Marketing, sales, and product operated with different readings of the same funnel, making coordinated experience evolution and consistent metric interpretation difficult."

problemSection:
  title: "Fragmented journey"
  description: "The acquisition experience treated hiring as a linear flow, without reflecting the real behavior of the B2B decision-maker. This created a misalignment between expectation and experience, with high drop-off rates in the early stages.\n\nThe lack of context and decision support hindered user progression, while mixed traffic increased noise in the journey. A significant part of the decision happened outside the product, without continuity within the digital experience."
  problems:
    - title: "Friction point"
      description: "Entry without context and low understanding of the value proposition"
    - title: "Technical limitation"
      description: "Rigid flow based on fixed steps, without adaptation by intent"
    - title: "Business goal"
      description: "Increase conversion in the self-service channel while maintaining operational efficiency"
darkSection:
  solutionTitle: "Nurturing and personalization"
  solutionDescription: "Personalization of the experience based on the stage of the purchasing process.\n\nThe solution originated from the understanding that acquisition is not an isolated moment, but part of a broader decision journey. The focus was to structure the experience to reflect different levels of intention and allow for contextual progression."
  strategyTitle: "Project Guidelines"
  strategies:
    - title: "Journey as a system"
      description: "Organize the experience considering multiple touchpoints and decisions"
    - title: "Intent as a driver"
      description: "Adapt paths based on the lead's maturity level"
    - title: "Progressive segmentation"
      description: "Refine user understanding throughout the journey"
    - title: "Contextual alignment"
      description: "Ensure coherence between message, timing, and expectation"
